## v34.11–v35.0 architecture consolidation

### v34.11 Review Window Clarity

- Strategic reviews are not rendered as pressure-window sentences.
- PART BUILD without a mapped upgrade remains PART BUILD; it does not imply passive/full-size approval.
- FULL BUILD without an upgrade review remains the active instruction.
- WAIT uses a dated re-entry review; PROTECT uses a dated protection review; active tactical states use the current active window.
- Decision instructions avoid unresolved `if`, `unless`, and `whether` language.

### v34.13 Re-entry Path + Core-Compounder Clarity

- Strategic WAIT stops at the dated re-entry review and the card is rerun there.
- The current WAIT instruction is not projected indefinitely beyond that gate.
- Part-sized strategic capital can be active even when no full-build/passive upgrade is mapped.
- Durable compounder pressure need not become a forced strategic exit when the astrological structure remains intact.

### v35.0 Holistic Astro-to-Capital Architecture

Production doctrine: **Astro Truth → Behaviour → Capital Decision → Timing Path → Language**. Each stage has one authority and may not rewrite an upstream result.

- `lib/v35/astroTruth.js`: astronomical facts, typed pressure, reliability, receptor fit and mapped dates; no capital language.
- `lib/v35/behaviour.js`: expected stock expression; no actions.
- `lib/v35/capitalDecision.js`: sole capital authority; independently answers existing position, fresh tactical, strategic and passive long-term capital.
- `lib/v35/timingPath.js`: now plus the next resolved astro gate only; rerun at that gate.
- `lib/v35/language.js`: deterministic rendering with no decision thresholds.
- `lib/v35/decisionPipeline.js`: ordered orchestration and inspectable decision trace.

Historical response enters only through a future structured `historicalContext`: old/current macro signatures, macro similarity, natal-contact similarity, episode-state similarity, observed behaviour distribution and sample quality. It may alter behaviour confidence/probability but may not rewrite Astro Truth, invent a break, reverse direction alone, select a capital action or override the next real gate.

Capital ladder: WAIT; PART BUILD; FULL BUILD; PASSIVE APPROVED. FULL BUILD is a reachable conviction zone and means building the intended position in stages, not an all-in single-price entry.

### v35.1 Window Sovereignty

- `decision_v35` is sovereign in the production table and expanded card; v34 decision functions are legacy fallbacks only.
- Catalyst proximity words such as `Prepare` are not capital instructions. Every upcoming catalyst is typed as Pressure Check, Expansion Review, or Catalyst Review, states the affected capital bucket, and says what is reviewed there.
- Tactical and strategic paths contain only Now, the next real astro gate, and Stop/Rerun. Generic weekly and monthly filler is suppressed for v35 cards.
- A distant cleanest window cannot block a productive present field. Eternal and Kaynes are explicit regression cases.
- Cycle runway magnitude, timing, and confidence are separate. The score is based on repeated productive/strong forward windows, leadership durability and break-window penalties; unconditional archetype and cluster lifts no longer saturate most stocks to EXTREME.

## HISTORICAL_EVENT_ARCHIVE_AUTOMATION.md

# Historical Event Archive Automation

## Objective

Build an empirical, reproducible archive for every credible natal chart of every stock. The archive must preserve complete sky context rather than treating one transit as an isolated cause.

## Unit of storage

The core record is an **episode-context snapshot**, not merely a transit date. Each record stores:

- stock and natal chart ID;
- transit family and continuous orb-entry/exact/orb-exit episode;
- all simultaneous natal contacts;
- macro pressure, expansion and volatility;
- macro events, clusters and overlaps;
- sector and natal archetype;
- prior episode state;
- forward returns and excursions at 5/10/20/30/60/90/180/365 days;
- maximum gain and drawdown;
- response class: acceleration, durable trend, tactical rally, reset, break, delayed break, repair or dormancy;
- data provenance and review status.

## Automated pipeline

1. Enumerate all built-in stocks and all credible natal candidates.
2. Reject any candidate chart for dates before its own activation date.
3. Scan daily or every three days for slow-planet and eclipse contacts.
4. Merge adjacent samples into continuous transit episodes.
5. At every episode peak and meaningful cluster transition, capture the entire macro+natal context vector.
6. Pull adjusted daily prices once per stock and cache locally.
7. Calculate forward response metrics and path shape.
8. Store append-only records with deterministic IDs.
9. Build searchable indices by company, chart, contact family, sector, macro regime, cluster, overlap and prior state.
10. Keep all historical outputs display-only until coverage and validation thresholds are met.

## Why not “every combination” as a literal Cartesian product

A literal archive of every planet × natal planet × aspect × daily macro combination would be enormous, highly duplicated and statistically noisy. The scalable alternative is:

- store continuous contact episodes;
- store full context vectors at peaks and state transitions;
- query similarity dynamically across those vectors.

This retains macro, cluster, overlap and natal interaction without manufacturing billions of redundant rows.

## Alternative/complement to historical analogues

A historical layer should not be the sole intelligence source. Use three complementary layers:

1. **Mechanistic episode engine:** current astrology, continuity, cycle phase and pressure role.
2. **Within-stock response memory:** how this natal chart historically converted comparable support/pressure families.
3. **Cross-sectional analogue archive:** similar sectors/archetypes under similar context vectors.

If historical coverage is weak, the model should explicitly fall back to the mechanistic engine rather than infer false confidence.

## Promotion gate

Historical evidence may influence production only when:

- enough within-company or high-quality cross-sectional episodes exist;
- price data is complete and adjusted;
- the query is multi-hypothesis rather than confirmatory;
- out-of-sample replay improves aggregate accuracy;
- existing control cases do not deteriorate.

## REPLAY_CAPITAL_HORIZON_GUARDRAILS_v31_5_3.md

# v31.5.3 — Capital Horizon & Window-Selector Guardrails

Status: replay/research only. Production scores, stock table, natal preference and action engine remain unchanged.

## Core doctrine

A stock can be tactically attractive, strategically conditional, and unsuitable for passive long-term capital at the same time. The replay layer must say this explicitly so a 30–60 day move is not confused with a 12–24 month holding thesis.

## Capital horizons

Every replay research reading now separates:

- Tactical 30–60 days: short defined move, recovery, acceleration burst or tactical lift.
- Strategic 3–12 months: capital deployment through the first major pressure gate.
- Passive long-term 12–24 months: longer runway through the cycle, or until a dated protection gate.

## Guardrails added

1. False dormancy veto
   - If the research layer sees an active episode, up/up-biased direction and no current dormancy, the replay should not call the current period dormant merely because a cleaner window comes later.
   - Correct language: active churn, selective participation, pressure-before-acceleration, or wait for cleaner trigger.

2. Cleanest-window override fix
   - A later cleanest/primary acceleration window cannot erase a productive current window.
   - Later clean windows should be labelled primary acceleration / stronger add window, not first accumulation opening, when current capital is already productive.

3. Break-horizon strategic gate
   - Major reset/break inside 0–6 months: tactical only; no fresh strategic capital.
   - Major reset/break inside 6–12 months: strategic conditional; protection date required.
   - Major reset/break beyond 12 months: do not suppress current strategic entry; show later-cycle warning.

4. Cycle-location governor
   - High expansion in early ignition can be build/enter.
   - High expansion in mature/late-cycle phase becomes hold winner / tactical only / protect later strength.
   - High support after a confirmed break begins as repair or re-ignition test, not automatic strategic accumulation.

5. Post-break inheritance
   - Once confirmedBreakDate exists, subsequent support stays repair/re-ignition until multiple supportive checkpoints, reduced pressure and recovered leadership/durability confirm a restored cycle.

6. Primary/secondary chart policy
   - Production should eventually use one primary validated chart per stock.
   - Secondary charts may add caution, earlier pressure warnings or acceleration confirmation, but should not create competing main recommendations.

7. Single-chart confidence limitation
   - Stocks with only one unvalidated chart should show provisional confidence and remain candidates for listing/restructuring-chart testing.

## Intended UI consequence

Replay panels now expose a capital-horizon block:

- Tactical 30–60d
- Strategic 3–12m
- Passive long-term 12–24m
- Cycle location
- Protection/break gate when mapped
- Window-selector guardrail status

These fields are display-only in v31.5.3.

## TRANSIT_RECEPTOR_MODEL_v32.md

# Transit Receptor Model — v32 TRM-1

This patch adds a company-specific expression layer above the existing v31.5 replay-validation architecture.

## Locked principle

Macro transit creates weather. Sector fit creates eligibility. Natal receptor creates expression. Historical replay creates confidence. Fundamentals create investability.

The model therefore does **not** allow a transit such as Jupiter in Cancer to become a universal bullish label.

## New engine

`lib/transitReceptorFitEngine.js`

Main export:

```js
evaluateTransitReceptorFit({ company, natal, transits, replay, macro, fundamentals })
```

## Output classes

- `RERATING_TRANSIT`
- `CONSTRUCTIVE_EXPRESSION`
- `SUPPORTIVE_BUT_MUTED`
- `CONTESTED_EXPANSION`
- `NON_RESPONSIVE_OR_ROTATION_AWAY`
- `PRESSURE_EXPRESSION`
- `PRESSURE_OR_ROTATION_AWAY`
- `WEAK_OR_BACKGROUND`

## Component scores

- `sectorThemeFit`
- `natalReceptorStrength`
- `supportNetworkQuality`
- `pressureInterference`
- `sectorRotationFit`
- `historicalEcho`
- `fundamentalTransmission`
- `natalReliability`
- `expressionScore`
- `confidenceScore`

## Hard guardrails

- No rerating label if natal receptor strength is below 60.
- No clean rerating label if pressure interference is above 55.
- No strong constructive label if sector fit is below 40 unless natal receptor is exceptional.
- Confidence is capped when natal reliability is weak.
- Investment-strength language is capped when fundamental transmission is weak.

## Acutaas / Lupin / BDL logic

The same Jupiter-in-Cancer weather can now map differently:

- Acutaas-style setup: high sector fit + strong natal receptor + support network = `RERATING_TRANSIT`
- Lupin-style setup: sector fit but weaker receptor/network or more pressure = `SUPPORTIVE_BUT_MUTED`
- BDL-style setup: weak Cancer sector fit and no exceptional natal compensation = `NON_RESPONSIVE_OR_ROTATION_AWAY` or pressure expression

## Wiring

The engine is now surfaced in:

- `lib/astroEngine.js` as `transit_receptor_fit`, `transit_receptor_expression`, `transit_receptor_score`, `transit_receptor_confidence`, and `transit_receptor_reading`
- `pages/api/replay.js` as `transitReceptorFit`
- `pages/api/replay-lab.js` as `transitReceptorFit` and inside `replaySummary`
- `pages/index.js` in stock detail and research rows

## Important implementation note

`fundamentalTransmission` is currently a lightweight placeholder using available company fields/text. For production, replace or enrich it with verified balance-sheet, earnings visibility, debt, capital intensity, and sector-demand fields.

## v34.7 Decision Architecture Audit — Tactical/Strategic Separation

This build locks the next architectural correction after the v34 simple/research table work. The audit conclusion is that the astrology engine is not the main weakness: the remaining issue is that tactical and strategic capital translation were still borrowing old mixed research strings. The simple table and card shape are retained, but the card now generates explicit tactical and strategic decision objects before rendering text.

### Universal audit findings

1. Tactical and strategic must be separate decision objects, not one combined action copied into two columns.
2. Pressure is not automatically a sell instruction. It can be noise, digestion, protection, or true break-risk.
3. Break-risk language is reserved for hard pressure: break pressure, hard eclipse/reset pressure, or high pressure that clearly dominates expansion with weak current leadership.
4. A future best accumulation date does not mean current deployment. If the best window is ahead, the current tactical action must be WATCH/REVIEW unless current leadership, score, dormancy, and pressure also qualify.
5. Week/month path tables must follow the same tactical/strategic decision objects as the simple card. They must not reintroduce generic “hold winner / stagger fresh capital” language into watch or dormancy cases.
6. Research details may show evidence, but should not overrule the simple story verdict.

### Tactical engine question

What should fresh or existing tactical capital do in the next 30–45 days?

Inputs: current leadership, tactical score, current pressure/expansion balance, dormancy, current/best window timing, and break-risk pressure.

Outputs: STAGGER ADD, WATCH CLOSELY, HOLD CORE, NO FRESH, TRIM SATELLITE, HEAVY TRIM.

### Strategic engine question

Is this a 3–12/18 month capital candidate, and at what size?

Inputs: strategic score, forward leadership durability, dormancy, pressure severity, current tactical usability, and long-cycle context.

Outputs: FULL BUILD, PART BUILD, WAIT, PROTECT, AVOID, NATAL PENDING.

### Reduction guardrail

TRIM/HEAVY TRIM/BREAK-RISK should not be triggered by generic pressure alone. If forward leadership or expansion structure remains visible and pressure is not true break-risk, the simple card should prefer NO FRESH / PRESSURE WATCH / PROTECT EXCESS over forced reduction. This addresses Cupid-type failures where pressure was translated into reduction too early.

### Forward review labels

Every future review must say what type of action is being reviewed: Expansion, Protection, Break-risk, Strategic, or Capital Review. No naked review dates.
